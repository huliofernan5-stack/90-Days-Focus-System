The Operating System

How this cycle works · Read once, then execute
The weekly structure
01

Monday Morning: Identity Priming

Before planning anything, write three sentences from the version of you who already had a good week. Set your three non-negotiables for the week. This takes five minutes and it changes everything that follows.

02

Each Morning: Three Core Tasks

Choose three tasks that genuinely matter today. Mark each Done, In Progress, Rescheduled, or Delegated. In Progress and Rescheduled carry the task forward to your next open slot automatically. Delegated removes it from your plate and lets you record who owns it. The core three are the floor — your minimum viable day.

03

Each Evening: One Sentence

Write one sentence about how you showed up today. Do this on hard days especially. The habit of honest self-observation is more durable than the habit you're tracking.

04

Sunday: Weekly Review

Each week has its own set of review prompts. They are not the same questions repeated they deepen as the cycle progresses. The review is where the work becomes learning. Do not skip it.

05

Weeks 4, 8 and 13: Deeper Pauses

Month-end check-ins at weeks 4 and 8 recalibrate your goals and your system. Week 13 closes with the full 90-day reflection. These are not optional. They are where the cycle earns its value.
